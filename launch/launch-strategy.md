# CampusClip — Western University Launch Strategy
## August 2026

*Execution document. This is not a strategy overview — it is a week-by-week, action-by-action plan.*

---

## The Core Problem: Cold Start

The social layer of CampusClip is worthless until enough students are already on it. A student opens the app, sees no classmates in their PSYCH 1000 section, and closes it forever. That is the failure mode. Every decision in this plan exists to prevent that outcome.

**The answer is not a slow rollout. It is a dense, fast, coordinated blitz on one campus.**

Target: 500–1,000 weekly active students at Western by end of September 2026. That means both the academic and social sides of the app in use — not just downloads.

The entire pre-launch phase is about seeding the network before the semester begins, so that when a first-year opens CampusClip on day one of orientation, they already see people they recognise in their classes.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (June–July 2026)

### What to Build Before You Show Up

**Product requirements for launch-readiness (must be done before August):**
- Syllabus photo-to-calendar flow must work flawlessly on the first try, every time. If this breaks during orientation week, the launch is over.
- Western University course catalogue pre-loaded so students can find their classes by department, course code, and section number.
- Class pages visible (and compelling) even with only 3–5 members — empty state design must communicate "be one of the first" not "nobody uses this."
- Push notification system live and tested — you need the ability to send targeted messages by class and by cohort.
- App Store and Google Play listings live with real screenshots, not wireframes.

**Analytics instrumentation (non-negotiable before launch):**
- Track: downloads, account creations, class joins, syllabus uploads, app opens day 2, day 7, day 14.
- North star metric: weekly active users who have used both academic tracking and the social layer in the same week.

---

### Who to Recruit in June–July

**The Class Rep Program (20–30 students)**

Recruit 20–30 returning Western students — one per major department — before the semester starts. These are your seeds. Without them, the network starts empty.

Targeting criteria:
- Second or third year, returning in September
- Involved: one or two clubs, present on campus, social but not loud about it
- Identifiable on Instagram through Western student hashtags, club pages, or Western's own student union account

How to recruit:
1. DM 100 students via Instagram in June. Message: "Hey — I'm building a student life app launching at Western in September. I'm looking for 20 students who want early access and a say in how it works on campus. 30 minutes of your time now, and you're on it before anyone else."
2. Run a Google Form to collect interest. Screen for genuine campus involvement, not follower count.
3. Onboard accepted reps over Zoom in July — walk them through the app, take their feedback seriously, fix things that matter.
4. Give them a concrete role: their job is to get 15–20 classmates on the app in the first two weeks of September. They are not brand ambassadors. They are the first people their friends trust.

**Why this works (psychology: social proof + network effects):** When a student sees that someone they already know — their lab partner, their floor mate — is already on CampusClip, the cold start objection disappears. The rep is the proof of life.

**What reps get:**
- Early access (weeks before anyone else)
- A direct line to the founder for feedback
- Their name in the credits / a "Founding Member" badge visible in their profile
- A case where they actually shaped how their campus uses a product — that is genuinely interesting to them

### Assets to Prepare (June–July)

**App Store listing:**
- Primary screenshot: a class page with 12 students, active chat, upcoming assignment visible. Not a feature grid. Show a living campus.
- Subtitle: "Your classes, your grades, your people."
- Description: see positioning-and-messaging.md for copy.

**Instagram presence:**
- Handle: @campusclip.western (campus-specific for launch, not generic)
- 6–8 posts before launch day showing real Western campus photos with captions that articulate the pain (not the product). "It's week two. You still don't know anyone in your lecture. You will." Post these in August before September.
- No product screenshots until orientation week.

**Street team kit:**
- 500 stickers (good stickers — matte finish, Western purple, no corporate logo, just the wordmark and "your campus, finally in one place")
- 200 business cards with a single QR code linking directly to the App Store page — no landing page, no email capture, straight to download
- 4 retractable banners for high-traffic locations
- 3 matching t-shirts for street team members (not branded polos — something students would actually wear)

**Email draft templates:**
- Outreach to Western professors (see below)
- Follow-up to class reps during launch week
- Trigger email sequence for new signups (day 0, day 3, day 7)

**Professor outreach (start in July):**
Target: 15–20 professors teaching large first and second-year courses (PSYCH 1000, BIOL 1001, ECON 1021, SOCI 1020, etc.). These are the courses where seeding 50 students in one class immediately makes the shared class system feel alive.

Email pitch:
> Subject: A question about PSYCH 1000 in the fall
>
> Hi Professor [Name],
>
> I'm building CampusClip — a student app launching at Western in September that helps students track grades and coordinate with classmates without creating a new group chat every semester.
>
> I'm not asking for a course integration or anything that requires your time. I'm asking if you'd be willing to mention the app to your class in the first week — something like "some of your classmates are using CampusClip to track this course" — so students know it exists.
>
> I'm happy to share what it does and how it works. Would a 15-minute call work?

Goal: 5–8 professors who will give a casual mention in the first week of class. That is enough.

**Campus media partnerships (July):**

## Phase 2: Launch Week — First Week of September 2026

*Orientation at Western University typically runs the last week of August through the first week of September. This plan assumes September 2–7 as the target window. Confirm exact dates and adjust accordingly.*

### The Principle

Be everywhere in the first five days. Every student who walks through Western's front gates in the first week should encounter CampusClip at least three times before they walk into their first class. That is the Rule of 7 compressed into a week.

---

### Day-by-Day Plan

**Day 1 (Tuesday, Sept 2) — Arrival and Move-In**

Where to be: Residence move-in zones — Elgin Hall, Saugeen-Maitland, Essex Hall. These are the highest foot-traffic moments of the entire year. Every first-year student passes through with their family.

What to do:
- 2 street team members per major residence zone from 9am–5pm
- Hand stickers to students, not parents. Say: "It's a student app for Western — tracks your grades and connects you with your classmates. It's free." Do not explain more than that. Let curiosity do the work.
- QR code on a retractable banner near the check-in tent if permission is granted (talk to ResLife in July)

**Day 2 (Wednesday, Sept 3) — Orientation Programming Begins**

Where to be: University Community Centre (UCC) Atrium — the central hub of all orientation activity.

What to do:
- Table in the UCC from 10am–4pm. Not a booth with a pitch — a table where class reps are sitting on their phones showing the app to friends who stop by.
- The street team is not pitching. They are facilitating: "My friend just showed me this — you can see your whole class in the app." Peer-to-peer, not brand-to-student.
- Post Instagram Story from @campusclip.western: "Orientation week at Western. If you just got here, open the App Store and search CampusClip. Your first class is already in there."

**Day 3 (Thursday, Sept 4) — Clubs Expo / Orientation Fair**

Western's orientation fair is the single best recruiting event of the year — hundreds of clubs in one room, every first-year student walks through. This is the highest-leverage moment of the week.

What to do:
- Secure a table at the fair (apply in June as soon as applications open — these spots fill fast)
- Table setup: laptop showing class pages that are already populated (by class reps who joined in advance), printed "your grade, always calculated" one-liner, QR code for download
- Message at the table: "You're going to be in five group chats by Monday. This is the one that's already connected to your classes."
- Collect first-hand feedback from students who stop — what confuses them, what delights them — and relay to the team in real time

**Day 4 (Friday, Sept 5) — Campus Saturation**

Where to be: Every high-traffic outdoor space. UC Hill, Concrete Beach, the walk from Weldon Library to the UCC.

What to do:
- Deploy all 3 street team members with stickers and QR cards
- Chalking on sidewalks (check with Western facilities for permission) — simple: "Your grade. Your class. Your campus. CampusClip."
- Class reps text their 15 target classmates today: "Hey — I've been using this app called CampusClip. It has our PSYCH 1000 class already set up. Download it before Monday."

**Day 5 (Saturday, Sept 6) — Residence Community Nights**

Most residences run community events this weekend. Talk to ResLife in July to get 5 minutes at one of these events — not to pitch, but to do a live demo. "I'm going to pull up your floor's class schedule and show you what it looks like when your whole floor is in the app."

This is the most intimate format and the most likely to convert to multi-use. A room of 30 first-years watching their classmates already in the app is the most powerful ad CampusClip can run.

**Day 6–7 (Sunday–Monday, Sept 7–8) — First Day of Classes**

This is the highest-stakes moment. Students are sitting in their first lectures. They are looking at their phones.

What to do:
- Class reps send a text to their classmate list that morning: "First day. If you're in ECON 1021 today, open CampusClip — I set up our section."
- If any professors agreed to give a mention, this is the week. A 30-second mention at the start of PSYCH 1000 with 400 students in the room is worth 400 individual street team conversations.
- Instagram post from @campusclip.western: "First day of class at Western. Some things you won't miss: calculating your grade in a spreadsheet. Open CampusClip before your second class."

## Phase 3: Post-Launch Momentum (October 2026)

By October 1, you should have 200–400 active students. The cold start is not fully solved yet — 500 WAUs is the threshold where word of mouth starts to carry. October is about closing that gap and building the infrastructure for Guelph/Laurier in January.

### Sustaining Momentum

**Class rep check-in (first week of October):**
Reconnect with all 20–30 class reps. Ask: what is working, what feels awkward, who needs more support? The reps who converted 15+ classmates in September are now informal community managers. Treat them that way. Give them a name — "Campus Leads" — and a group chat where they talk to each other.

**Midterm push (mid-October):**
Midterms are the single highest-motivation moment for academic engagement. By week 6–7 of semester, students are calculating what they need on the midterm to get a B in the course. CampusClip solves this exactly.

Tactics:
- Instagram Story series: "Two weeks until midterms. This is what your grade looks like right now in CampusClip." (Show a real grade tracker UI — blur the name, keep the numbers.)
- Push notification to all users week 5: "Midterms are in 10 days. Your grade is already calculated. Open CampusClip."
- Street team reactivation at the library — Weldon and Taylor — the week before midterms begin. This is when students are most open to anything that makes their academic life easier.

**Club partnership deepening:**
By October, student clubs are fully active. Identify 5–10 clubs whose membership overlaps with your target user (GPA-conscious, socially active, involved). Offer to list their events in CampusClip for free. Ask for a mention in their newsletter or Instagram Stories in exchange. Target: Western Investment Club, HBSA, Pre-Med Society, Western Engineering Society.

**Word-of-mouth reinforcement:**
By October, some students are naturally telling their friends about CampusClip. Make it easier. Add a "Share with your classmates" prompt that appears after a student uploads their syllabus — the highest-engagement moment in the product. The prompt should be specific: "3 students in your BIOL 1001 section haven't joined yet. Invite them."

---

### Building Toward Guelph and Laurier (November–December 2026)

The expansion to Guelph and Laurier in January 2027 follows the same playbook — but with one advantage: you have proof. By November, you have 500+ WAUs at Western, real testimonials, and class reps who can speak to the experience. That is the foundation for the next launch.

Actions in November–December:
- Identify 5–10 Guelph and Laurier students via Instagram (look for students who have tagged Western-adjacent events or communities — transfer students, friends of Western students)
- Run the same class rep recruitment process in December — onboard 10–15 students per campus before January
- Prepare a "CampusClip is expanding" Instagram post for early January that Western's existing users can share — this is the borrowed channel moment for the Guelph/Laurier launch

## What Success Looks Like

### 30 Days (End of September)

- 300–500 downloads at Western
- 200+ weekly active users (not just downloads)
- At least 50 class pages created with 5+ members each
- At least 20 syllabus uploads in the top 5 large first-year courses
- Average session per week: 2+ opens without a push notification
- 5 pieces of unprompted word-of-mouth documented (students texting friends, posting to Stories, etc.)

### 60 Days (End of October)

- 500–750 weekly active users
- Students using both academic (grade tracking, syllabus upload) and social (class chat, event discovery) in the same week — this is the north star
- At least 3 class pages where the chat is active without any prompting from the team
- App Store rating 4.3 or above with at least 50 reviews
- Midterm period driving a measurable spike in opens (proof that the academic use case has real retention)

### 90 Days (End of November)

- 750–1,000 weekly active users at Western
- Word-of-mouth is measurable: new signups citing "a friend told me" in onboarding survey
- Class rep program operating semi-independently — leads are recruiting their own replacements for January
- Guelph and Laurier pre-launch cohorts recruited (10–15 students each)
- One piece of earned media published (Western Gazette article, CHRW interview, or equivalent)
- The cold start problem is solved: any new student who joins a class with 10+ members experiences the social layer as alive on day one
