# CampusClip Ad Creative
## 10 Meta/Instagram Ad Variations — Western University Launch

---

**Brand voice reminder:** Casual, direct, peer-to-peer. Never corporate. Short sentences. Talk to students like a friend who's the most organised person they know. Words to use: scattered, all in one place, your classes, actually, just, finally, your grade. Words to avoid: productivity, optimise, leverage, seamless, solution, platform, empower, revolutionise.

---

## Ad 1 — The Chaos Angle

**Angle:** You have too many apps open and none of them talk to each other.

**Headline:** 6 apps. One university.

**Primary text:**
Canvas for your assignments. Google Calendar you stopped updating in week 2. A group chat that buried the due date. Notes on your phone from that one lecture. Instagram DMs to find your classmate's number.

All of it for one semester of university.

CampusClip puts your classes, grades, assignments, and classmates in one place. Photograph your syllabus. Done.

**CTA:** Download Free

**Visual description:** Split screen. Left side: a phone screen overwhelmed with app notification badges — Canvas, Google Calendar, WhatsApp, Instagram, Notes, Reminders, all piled up. Right side: clean CampusClip interface showing a single class view with assignment due dates, grade tracker, and classmate avatars. Simple, not flashy.

**Format:** Instagram Feed (1:1) and Story (9:16 version crops to the phone comparison)

**Audience:** Western University geo, 18–23, broad interest targeting. Best cold audience ad — runs throughout all three phases.

Not your last assignment. Your actual grade. With all the weights, what you've done so far, and how much is left.

Most students don't know. They wait for Canvas to update. Or they do the math themselves and hope they got it right.

CampusClip calculates it for you, automatically, the whole time. Photograph your syllabus once. Your grade is always there.

**CTA:** Download Free

**Visual description:** Close-up of a phone screen showing a grade tracker: course name (PSYCH 1000), current grade displayed prominently (73.4%), with a clean breakdown — midterm 40%, quiz 10%, final 50%, and a bar showing how much of the semester is completed. The grade feels alive, not static.

**Audience:** Western University geo, 18–23, layered with academic app interest and "students" behavioural signal. High-intent creative — best for retargeting students who visited the app store but didn't install.

**Headline:** Photograph your syllabus. Your semester populates.

**Primary text:**
First week of classes. You've got four syllabi. Every one has assignments, weights, and due dates buried in a PDF.

Photograph them with CampusClip. Every assignment goes into your calendar. Every weight goes into your grade tracker. You don't type anything.

That's your semester, organised in about two minutes.

**CTA:** See How It Works

**Visual description:** Overhead shot: a student's hand holding a phone over a printed syllabus on a desk. The next frame shows the CampusClip app with the assignments and due dates populated — same information, now clean and sorted by date. Real-feeling, not produced.

**Format:** Instagram Reels (9:16), 15-second video. First 3 seconds: hand raising phone over syllabus. Seconds 3–10: syllabus uploads, assignments populate. Seconds 10–15: grade tracker shows up with everything calculated.

**Audience:** Western University geo, 18–21 (first and second-year focus), back-to-school interest signal. Run in Phase 1 (pre-launch) and Phase 2 (launch week).

And it calculates your grade the whole time so you always know where you stand.

**CTA:** Download Free

**Visual description:** Low-fi, almost looks organic. Text on a plain background, like someone typed it. Or: a phone screen screenshot style, as if you're reading a text message from a friend. The "ad" quality is intentionally minimal. Feels discovered, not pushed.

**Format:** Instagram Story (9:16), TikTok text-style video

**Audience:** Western University geo, 18–22, cold audience. Run in Phase 2 and Phase 3. The informal tone specifically counteracts ad fatigue — when students have seen the polished ads, this one feels different because it doesn't look like an ad at all.

## Creative Testing Priority

Run these ads in this order based on phase:

**Phase 1 (Pre-launch, July 28 – August 10):**
Start with Ads 1, 3, and 5. These build awareness without requiring social proof that doesn't exist yet.

**Phase 2 (Launch week, August 11 – August 24):**
Run all 10. Prioritise Ads 2, 4, 7. This is when install volume matters most — test everything and cut the bottom two performers by day 5.

**Phase 3 (Post-launch, August 25 – September 30):**
Refresh with Ads 6, 8, 9, 10. Retire anything showing frequency fatigue (frequency above 4 on Meta). Re-engagement ads (Ads 6 and 9) run to dormant installs via retargeting; new acquisition ads (Ads 8 and 10) run to cold audiences who haven't seen CampusClip yet.

## Copy Notes for Production

- All primary text is written to front-load the hook — the first sentence is the most important because it decides whether the student keeps reading or scrolls past.
- CTAs are "Download Free" on acquisition ads and "See How It Works" on awareness ads. Never "Learn More" — too passive for this audience.
- Character counts are within Meta's recommended limits. Primary texts are longer than the 125-character visible window — Meta truncates with "... more." The first 100–125 characters must stand alone as a complete hook.
- Video descriptions are written as production briefs — hand to a student creator or shoot in-house. Polish is not the goal. A student with an iPhone can produce the right feel for Ads 5 and 10 in one afternoon.
- All visuals should feel like they were made by a student, not by an agency. Stock photography will not work for this audience.
