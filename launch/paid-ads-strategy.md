# CampusClip Paid Media Launch Plan
## Western University — August–September 2026

---

## Overview

**Goal:** 500–1,000 weekly active students at Western by end of September 2026.
**Primary conversion action:** App download + account creation.
**Campaign window:** July 28 – September 30, 2026.
**Critical constraint:** Launch density beats launch breadth. Every dollar should funnel Western students into the app, not create broad Canadian awareness.

---

## Channel Strategy

### Primary: Meta (Instagram)

Instagram is where this audience lives. Western students are 19–21, heavily visual, and spend more time in Instagram than any other social app. Meta's geo and university-level interest targeting is the most precise available for this use case.

**Why Meta wins for CampusClip:**
- Granular geo-targeting down to Western's campus radius
- Interest and behavioral signals that identify enrolled students (student apps, back-to-school shopping, academic tools)
- Stories and Reels are native to how students consume content — UGC-style creative performs here without looking like an ad
- Retargeting allows follow-up to anyone who clicks but doesn't download

**Budget allocation: 60% of total spend**

---

### Secondary: TikTok

TikTok is where the chaos angle lives. The "you have six apps open right now" concept is built for short-form video — students already make content about the overwhelm of managing their academic life. CampusClip's syllabus-to-calendar feature is genuinely visual and demonstrable in 15 seconds.

TikTok skews slightly younger (18–20) which overlaps well with first and second-year students at Western — the highest-value early adopters.

**Why TikTok:**
- Problem-demo-reveal format is the native TikTok format
- Students already follow the "student life struggle" genre — CampusClip fits that feed naturally
- Cost-per-install typically lower than Meta for 18–22 demographic
- Organic amplification possible if a video catches traction

**Budget allocation: 30% of total spend**

---

### Tertiary: Reddit (small test only)

r/uwo is a real and active community where Western students ask real questions, complain about Canvas, coordinate classes, and share things that are actually useful. A Reddit presence is worth testing but requires a different approach — native-feeling, useful, never promotional.

Reddit ads in r/uwo can look like posts. If the creative is good enough to look like something a student posted, it works. If it looks like an ad, it gets downvoted into irrelevance.

**Why Reddit (conditional):**
- Hyper-targeted to r/uwo specifically — no wasted spend
- High-intent audience (people actively discussing the exact problems CampusClip solves)
- Can test authentic "useful info" ad formats that don't look like ads

**Budget allocation: 10% of total spend — pause if CPA is poor in first 2 weeks**

---

## Targeting Strategy

### Meta — How to Reach Western University Students

**Geographic targeting:**
- London, Ontario, Canada
- 3km radius from Western University's main campus (University College)
- This radius catches on-campus housing, Masonville area, student corridors
- Exclude downtown London outside student corridors to avoid wasted impressions

**Demographic layer:**
- Age: 18–23
- Gender: All
- Language: English

**Interest and behavioral targeting (layer these, don't stack exclusively):**
- Interests: University/college student life, Campus life, Back to school, Academic planning apps
- Behaviors: Recent mobile app users, Frequent app installers (signals someone who tries new apps)
- Education: In university/college (current students signal)

**University-specific targeting:**
- Use "Education > Currently enrolled at > Western University" where available in Meta's education targeting
- If Meta doesn't surface Western directly, layer London + 18–23 + university interests — this audience is highly correlated

**Lookalike audience (Phase 2 and beyond):**
- Once the first 50–100 users have downloaded, build a 1% lookalike from the conversion event list
- This is the most scalable lever once early data exists

**Exclusions:**
- Anyone who has already installed the app (exclude from acquisition campaigns)
- Age 24+ (reduces wasted spend on grad students and non-students)

---

### TikTok — Targeting

**Geographic:** London, Ontario — 3km radius from Western, same logic as Meta.

**Age:** 18–22.

**Interest categories:** Education, Student life, College/University.

**Behavioral:** Users who engage with study-related, campus-life, and back-to-school content.

**TikTok-specific:** Use the "App Installs" objective — TikTok's algorithm optimizes toward people who install apps within the targeted interest cluster. Feed it 3–5 creative variations and let the algorithm find the winners in the first week.

---

### Reddit — Targeting

**Subreddit targeting:** r/uwo specifically. This is non-negotiable — do not run broader Canadian university targeting.

**Ad format:** Promoted post that looks like organic content. Subject line written like a student post, not an ad headline.

## Budget Recommendation

### Total Recommended Budget: $3,000–$5,000 CAD for August–September

This is a modest budget for a hyper-targeted single-campus launch. The small geography (London, ON) and narrow age band means CPMs are very manageable — you're not competing for national inventory.

**Phase breakdown across budget:**

| Phase | Timing | Budget % | Amount ($4,000 mid-range) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-launch awareness | July 28 – Aug 10 | 20% | $800 |
| Launch week push | Aug 11 – Aug 24 | 40% | $1,600 |

**Channel split within each phase:**

| Channel | % of Phase Budget |
|---|---|
| Meta/Instagram | 60% |

## Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch Awareness
**Dates: July 28 – August 10, 2026**
**Objective: Awareness + click traffic (not installs yet)**

Students haven't gone back to campus yet but they're thinking about the new year. This is when anxiety about getting organised starts. Hit them before they've made their decisions about which apps to use.

**Goal for this phase:**
- Build brand recognition before arrival week
- Drive curiosity traffic to app store listing or landing page
- Collect pixel data for retargeting

**Ads to run:**
- Chaos angle: "You're about to have 6 apps open at once. There's a better way."
- Syllabus angle: "What if your whole schedule populated on day one?"
- Tease format — don't give away everything. Make them want to know what the app does.

**Targeting:** Broad Western geo + university interest stack. No lookalikes yet (no conversion data).

**KPIs:**
- CPM under $15 CAD
- Link CTR above 1.5%
- App store page visits: 200+ over the phase

---

### Phase 2: Launch Week Push
**Dates: August 11 – August 24, 2026**
**Objective: App Installs (primary)**

This is O-Week and the first week of classes. This is the single highest-value window in the entire calendar — students are looking for everything at once, open to new apps, and in the habit-formation period that will define how they manage their year.

Every dollar should go toward installs this week.

**Goal for this phase:**
- Maximum install volume at lowest possible CPA
- Hit first 200–300 active users to seed the social layer
- Create enough campus density that early users see classmates already there

**Ads to run:**
- All four main angles (chaos, grade anxiety, social discovery, syllabus hook)
- Multiple creative variations per angle — at least 2 per ad set
- Story ads + feed ads running simultaneously

**Targeting:**
- Retarget everyone who visited the app store page but didn't convert (from Phase 1)
- New audience acquisition: Western geo + university targeting
- TikTok app install campaigns fully active

**Bid strategy:**
- Meta: Optimize for app installs, cost cap at $5–8 CAD per install
- TikTok: App Install objective, lowest cost initially to gather data

**KPIs:**
- CPI (cost per install): Under $6 CAD
- 300+ installs in the first 2 weeks
- Install-to-activation rate (account creation): above 60%

---

### Phase 3: Post-Launch Retention Ads
**Dates: August 25 – September 30, 2026**
**Objective: Re-engagement + continued acquisition**

Most students who downloaded and activated are in habit-forming territory. Ads in this phase do two jobs: (1) keep bringing in new installs from students who missed the first wave, and (2) retarget students who downloaded but haven't opened the app in 7+ days.

**Sub-campaigns:**

**New acquisition (60% of phase budget):**
- Word-of-mouth angle: "Everyone in your PSYCH 1000 lecture is on CampusClip"
- Grade anxiety angle: "Three weeks in. Do you know your grade?"
- Fresher creative — previous launch week ads may be showing fatigue; swap in new variations

**Retargeting / re-engagement (40% of phase budget):**
- Target: App installs who haven't triggered key in-app events (syllabus upload, class join)
- Message: "You downloaded it. Photograph your syllabus in 30 seconds."
- Message: "Your grade is calculating itself. Check it."

**KPIs:**
- New installs: 200–400 additional through September
- Re-engagement rate: 20%+ of dormant installs open app within 7 days of retarget ad
- WAU at Western: 500+ by September 30

## KPIs and Success Metrics

### Primary Metric
**Weekly Active Users at Western University by September 30, 2026: 500–1,000**

This is the only metric that proves the launch worked. Downloads don't prove the social layer is alive. WAU does.

---

### Campaign-Level KPIs

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per install (CPI) | Under $6 CAD | Blended across Meta + TikTok |
| Install-to-activation rate | 60%+ | Account creation after install |
| Link CTR (Meta) | 1.5–2.5% | Signals creative resonance |
| App store page CTR | 3%+ | From ad to app store |
| Re-engagement rate | 20%+ | Dormant users who return after retargeting |
| CPM | Under $15 CAD | On London, ON geo with narrow age |

---

### Phase-Specific Success Signals

**Phase 1 (Pre-launch):**
- 200+ app store page visits
- Pixel audience of 500+ for retargeting pool
- Any organic shares or DMs = strong signal

**Phase 2 (Launch week):**
- 300+ installs in the first 2 weeks
- Students finding classmates in-app within 48 hours of joining classes
- Zero major app stability issues

**Phase 3 (Post-launch):**
- WAU holds or grows week-over-week without requiring ad spend to spike
- Ratio of organic installs (non-paid) starts increasing — this signals word-of-mouth kicking in
- Users using both academic and social features (the product's core value is compound)

---

## What "Good" Looks Like at 30 Days

By September 10, the signal you want is: students are finding their classmates in-app, the social layer feels populated, and new installs are telling you "I heard about it from someone in my class." That's the inflection point. Before that inflection, paid ads are doing the heavy lifting. After it, paid ads become acceleration, not foundation.

If by September 10 the WAU is under 200 and installs are coming in but not activating both sides of the app, the problem is onboarding and in-app experience — not ad spend. Doubling the budget won't fix that.
