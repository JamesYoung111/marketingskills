# CampusClip — 90-Day Content Strategy
**Period:** August – October 2026
**Campus:** Western University
**Goal:** 500–1,000 weekly active users by end of September 2026

---

## Strategic Foundation

### The One Thing This Strategy Must Solve

The cold start problem. CampusClip's social layer is only valuable when classmates are on it. Students opening the app for the first time need to feel like their campus is already alive — not like they're walking into an empty room.

That means density beats breadth at every stage. One post that reaches 500 Western students is worth more than five posts that each reach 100 students from five different campuses. Every content decision should ask: does this reach Western students specifically, or does it reach a vague "university student" audience that won't help network density on campus?

### What Success Looks Like

- Downloads are vanity. Opens are the metric.
- Target: students opening the app without a push notification.
- Target: students using both the academic and social sides in the same week.
- Target: a student telling a friend "you should get on this" before the end of September.

## The Three Phases

---

## Phase 1 — Awareness (August 1–31)
### Make Western students aware CampusClip exists before semester starts

**The goal:** Build an audience of Western students on Instagram and TikTok who are primed to download on day one of semester — or ideally before it.

**The challenge:** You have no users yet, which means no social proof, no screenshots of real activity, and no "your classmate is already on it" hook. The content has to earn attention on the problem, not the product.

---

### Content Pillars — Phase 1

#### Pillar 1: The Fragmentation Problem (35% of content)

Western students are already frustrated. They just haven't named it. This pillar names the problem for them — the scattered feeling of managing university life across six apps that don't talk to each other. No product pitch. Just recognition.

The goal is for a student to see it and think "that's exactly my life," not "that's an ad."

Content types:
- Meme-style relatable statics showing the 6-app chaos (Instagram)
- "POV: it's week 6 and you still don't know anyone in your lecture" reels (TikTok)
- Group chat screenshots memes — "wait when is the assignment due" (Instagram)
- Relatable scenario reels: week 3 and already feeling behind (TikTok)

Voice cue: never blame the student. The tools are the problem, not them.

Example caption:
> you have 6 apps open and still don't know when your midterm is
>
> canvas. google cal. your notes app. the group chat you're searching through. instagram dms. another group chat.
>
> and somehow you still have to ask "wait when is the assignment due"
>
> we made an app for this. CampusClip drops at Western this September.

---

#### Pillar 2: Feature Revelation (30% of content)

Show what CampusClip actually does — but through the lens of "this problem is solved," not "here are our features." Every feature post should start with the pain, not the product.

The three moments to highlight, in order of relatability:
1. Syllabus upload → auto-populate (most visual, most wow)
2. Weighted grade always visible (most emotionally resonant)
3. Class social layer — classmates without Instagram hunting (most novel)

Content types:
- "Did you know" carousels (Instagram) — reveal each feature as a thing most students don't have
- "Watch me set up my semester in 30 seconds" demo reels (TikTok) — syllabus scan moment is the hero
- Feature highlight carousels showing all three features together (Instagram)
- "Three things CampusClip does that nothing else does" (Instagram)

Example caption (TikTok):
> watch me set up my entire semester in under a minute
>
> photograph syllabus. watch every assignment, weight, and due date appear automatically. that's it. that's your whole semester organized before the first lecture.
>
> dropping at Western in September. link in bio.

---

#### Pillar 3: Social Proof and Credibility (20% of content)

Pre-launch social proof is real — it just has to be honest. Beta feedback is available. The product was tested with a friend group and the validation was strong. Use that.

Testimonial-style posts work even before wide launch if they're sourced from real beta users and framed accurately (not "thousands of students love it" — just one real quote from a real person).

Content types:
- Quote cards with real beta quotes: "my whole university life was just there" (Instagram)
- "I stopped feeling one step behind" testimonial style (Instagram)
- Founder story reel — why this was built, what the real problem is (TikTok)

The founder reel is a Phase 1 asset with long tail. A real person talking to camera about why they built something for their own campus is more trustworthy than any polished ad. Film it once, use it throughout August.

Example caption:
> "my whole university life was just there"
>
> that's a real quote from a real person who used CampusClip in beta.
>
> launching at Western University this September. follow for updates.

---

#### Pillar 4: Countdown and Urgency (15% of content)

The closer to September, the more explicitly the content can count down and create FOMO around being an early adopter. "The students who get on first week are going to have a different year" — not because it's dramatic, but because the social layer genuinely gets more useful as more classmates join.

Posts: Aug 7 (25 days out), Aug 14 (18 days), Aug 21 (11 days), Aug 28 (4 days).

Content types:
- Bold typography countdown statics (Instagram)
- "3 weeks until semester — here's what changes" reels (TikTok)
- Early adopter framing: "the students who get on it in week one..." (TikTok)

---

### Posting Cadence — Phase 1

**Instagram:** 1 post per day, 5–6 days per week
- Mix: 2 reels, 2 carousels, 1–2 statics per week
- Stories: daily — repurpose feed content, polls ("do you know your actual grade rn?"), countdowns

**TikTok:** 1 post per day, 5 days per week
- All video: mix of talking head, screen demo, text-on-screen, and relatable scenario formats
- Comment engagement in first hour is critical — respond to every comment in the first 60 minutes

**Ratio:** 70% problem/relatable content, 30% product content in August

---

### Cold Start Content Solutions — Phase 1

The cold start problem hits content too. No one wants to follow an account with 0 posts and 40 followers. These tactics build early credibility:

**1. Batch-post the first week.** Launch with 7 posts already up. When the account launches August 1, it should already look active. The first visit should feel like there's something here.

**2. Borrow audience with Western-specific hashtags.** Every post uses #WesternU, #UWO, #WesternUniversity — these are existing communities on Instagram. Western students already browse these tags. Using them is borrowing existing attention.

**3. Lead with the problem, not the product.** Posts that name a real student problem get shared by students who relate to it. Shared content builds the audience organically among exactly the right people — other Western students.

**4. Comment-seed on Western content.** Identify 10–15 active Western University accounts (Western orientation pages, Western clubs with active Instagram, Western-adjacent TikTok creators). Follow them. Comment genuinely on their posts. Be the "did anyone else notice this?" voice, not "check out our app."

**5. The referral post.** Mid-August, one post explicitly says: "if you know a Western student, send them this." Don't ask for likes. Ask for shares to a specific person. This one post, if it lands, can seed the audience better than any ad.

---

### Phase 1 Metrics to Watch

- Instagram follower growth (target: 300+ Western students by August 31)
- TikTok reach per video (target: 1,000+ views per video from Canadian accounts)
- Profile visits → link clicks (this is the pre-download funnel)
- Story poll response rates (tells you which pain points land hardest)
- Comments that say "this is literally me" or "I need this" — qualitative signal of resonance

## Phase 2 — Activation (September 1–30)
### Drive downloads in the first week of semester

**The goal:** Convert the August audience into day-one downloads. Get students onto CampusClip in the first two weeks of semester — before habits for the new year are set.

**The insight:** The window for new habit adoption is the first two weeks of semester. Students are in "new year, new me" mode. They're setting up their systems. They're still deciding which apps make the cut. After week two, habits calcify.

---

### Content Shift for September

The voice shifts from "something is coming" to "it's here, get on it." But the tone stays peer-to-peer — never a launch announcement, always a recommendation from a friend.

The content ratio flips: 60% product/how-to, 40% relatable/social proof.

---

### Content Pillars — Phase 2

#### Pillar 1: Day One Setup Content (40% of content)

The single best activation post is a "watch me set up my semester in 60 seconds" reel filmed on the first day of class. Real campus. Real syllabus. Real setup. This is the post that drives downloads because it makes the value concrete and immediate.

Post this September 1 or the day classes begin — whichever comes first. Then repost variations weekly through the first two weeks.

Content types:
- "Day one setup" reel — syllabus scan, auto-populate, grade tracker ready (TikTok + Instagram Reel)
- "First day of class and I'm already set up" caption style
- "My semester is organised before the first lecture" carousel (Instagram)

Example caption:
> first day of class. opened CampusClip, photographed my syllabus, and every assignment just appeared in my calendar.
>
> I've been a student for two years and this is the first time I've felt set up before anything was even due.
>
> it's free. it's at Western. you should be on it.

---

#### Pillar 2: Real-Time Social Proof (30% of content)

As soon as real students are using it, capture their reactions. Not formal testimonials — casual, real, unpolished. A student saying "okay this is actually sick" in a comment is more powerful than a produced quote card.

If you can get even five students to share short videos or Stories using the app in the first week, that content is worth more than any planned post. Reshare it. Put it on Stories. Make early adopters visible.

Content types:
- Story reposts of early users
- "students are already on it" — screenshot of real usage numbers (even if small — "47 Western students joined this week" is more credible than a round number)
- Comment-based social proof — screenshot a real comment and post it as a Story

---

#### Pillar 3: Use-Case Walkthroughs (20% of content)

As students encounter specific problems in the first weeks of semester, post solutions in real time. The assignment is posted on Canvas → post "here's how to sync it in CampusClip." Someone asks about group projects → post "here's the class social layer."

These posts are useful, not promotional. They feel like tips from a classmate, not a product tutorial.

Content types:
- "Here's how to find your classmates on CampusClip" walkthrough (TikTok)
- "This is how to read your grade before Canvas updates" demo (TikTok)
- "Week 1 and I already know everyone in my class" carousel (Instagram)

---

#### Pillar 4: FOMO and Peer Pressure (10% of content)

Lean into the network effect honestly. The app is more useful as more classmates join. Saying so is not manipulative — it's true.

Content types:
- "The more people from your class who join, the more useful it gets" posts
- "Your classmates are setting up their semesters. are you?" (countdown/urgency format)
- "You've been on it for a week — send this to one friend in your class" (referral prompt)

---

### September Posting Cadence

**Instagram:** 1–2 posts per day, 6 days per week
- Heavy Reels and Carousels
- Daily Stories with polls and Q&A ("what's your biggest first-week problem?")

**TikTok:** 1–2 posts per day, 7 days per week in week 1 of semester
- The algorithm rewards consistency in bursts — post heavily week 1, then sustain at 1/day
- Respond to every comment in first 60 minutes

**Ratio:** 60% activation/product, 40% relatable/social proof

---

### The First-Week Download Push

The activation event is the first week of semester. Structure content around it:

**September 1–2 (Pre-launch hype):**
> "Western semester starts in 3 days. if you're on CampusClip, you're already set up before you walk into your first class."

**September 3–5 (Semester starts):**
Real-time content: "day one at Western — here's what happens when you're already set up." Film on campus if possible. Make it feel live.

**September 6–12 (Week 1):**
Daily posts showing real use: grade already tracked, classmates already connected, no new group chat needed. This week is the highest-frequency week of the entire 90 days.

**September 13–30 (Sustain):**
Settle into 1/day cadence. Content shifts toward community building — who's using it, what they're saying, what they've discovered.

### Phase 2 Metrics

- Downloads (primary conversion)
- Account creations (secondary — download without signup is a missed activation)
- DAU/WAU in first two weeks (are they coming back?)
- Content shares per post (social-proof content should get shared peer-to-peer)

## Phase 3 — Retention (October 1–31)
### Keep students engaged and sharing

**The goal:** Turn downloaders into habitual users who open the app without a prompt — and who recommend it without being asked.

**The insight:** The students who are still active in October are the product's real salespeople for January expansion to Guelph and Laurier. Retention in October is growth marketing for 2027.

---

### The Retention Problem

Students download apps constantly and abandon them just as fast. CampusClip's retention advantage is that it's tied to the academic calendar — the semester gives it a built-in reason to come back. The content strategy should reinforce the habit by making the app feel like it's part of the student's weekly rhythm, not something to try once.

Retention content is not "come back to the app." It's content that's useful or fun enough that students check the account (and by extension, the app) as a matter of habit.

---

### Content Pillars — Phase 3

#### Pillar 1: Community and Campus Life (35% of content)

In October, the content stops being about the app and starts being about the students using it. Western campus life content — events, shared experiences, relatable mid-semester moments — builds community around the account. Students follow the account because it reflects their experience, not just because it's a product page.

Content types:
- "It's midterm season and everyone on CampusClip is feeling it" (relatable)
- Western campus event highlights — things happening this week
- "What's your grade right now — be honest" Story polls
- "Most relatable CampusClip moment this week" — sourced from users

---

#### Pillar 2: Retention Triggers and Habit Reinforcement (25% of content)

Post content that gives students a reason to open the app right now. A poll about grades. A reminder that the app calculated their midterm score already. A "have you checked your grade this week" prompt.

These are engagement posts but they're also activation posts — someone who's lapsed sees it and comes back.

Content types:
- "You haven't checked your grade since week 3. it's week 6. open CampusClip." (TikTok)
- "It's midterm week — here's what CampusClip tells you about where you stand" (demo)
- "Your classmates just coordinated for the group project. did you?" (social layer activation)
- Story countdown to major academic deadlines ("add/drop deadline is in 3 days")

---

#### Pillar 3: User Stories and Peer Advocacy (25% of content)

The best retention marketing is a real student saying "I actually use this every week." Find the students who are genuine advocates — the ones who open it without prompting, who've told a friend, who've used both sides of the app — and give them a platform.

This content is also acquisition for anyone not yet on the app. A third-party recommendation converts better than any first-party post.

Content types:
- "How I use CampusClip mid-semester" student-style reel (TikTok)
- DM screenshots (with permission): "okay this is actually so useful"
- "Which feature do you use most?" Story poll with results shared
- Student case study format: before CampusClip vs. now (carousel)

---

#### Pillar 4: Forward Momentum and January Tease (15% of content)

In late October, start seeding the Guelph and Laurier expansion. This has two effects: it validates that CampusClip is real and growing (not just a Western experiment), and it creates urgency for Western students to deepen their engagement before their friends at other schools are on it.

Content types:
- "We're growing. new campuses coming January 2027." (announcement)
- "If you have a friend at Guelph or Laurier — this is for them." (referral seed)
- "You were first at Western. spread the word." (identity play — early adopter pride)

---

### October Posting Cadence

**Instagram:** 5–6 posts per week
- 2 Reels, 2 Carousels, 1–2 Stories-first content (polls, Q&As, countdowns)
- Pull back from daily posting — quality over frequency as the initial launch sprint ends

**TikTok:** 5–7 posts per week
- More user-story and community content
- Continue responding to every comment — this is now a community management task as much as a content task

**Ratio:** 35% community/relatable, 35% user stories/social proof, 15% retention triggers, 15% product/forward momentum

---

### How to Handle Retention Drops

If weekly active users plateau or decline in October, these are the levers:

**In-app trigger:** Push notification tied to academic calendar event ("your midterm is in 3 days — here's where you stand"). Not a generic "come back" notification. A relevant, grade-aware one.

**Content trigger:** Post something so relatable it gets shared within the Western student community on its own. The best performing relatable posts can pull lapsed users back through organic discovery.

**Peer trigger:** Activate the top 10–20 most engaged users as informal ambassadors. DM them. Tell them the app is growing. Ask for their honest feedback. Students who feel like insiders become advocates without being asked.

## Platform Priorities

### Instagram — Primary

Western students are on Instagram. Clubs are on Instagram. The audience that makes CampusClip's social layer work is already discoverable on Instagram via #WesternU.

Instagram is the awareness and social proof platform. Carousels drive saves (algorithm signal). Reels drive reach. Stories drive daily engagement and habit formation.

**Key tactics:**
- Always include #WesternU, #UWO, #WesternUniversity on every post
- Use location tags: London, Ontario; Western University
- Stories polls build micro-habits around the account (check in daily for results)
- Carousel saves signal the algorithm — "did you know" and feature carousels are designed to be saved

### TikTok — Primary (equal weight to Instagram)

TikTok's algorithm distributes content to users regardless of follower count — which matters enormously for a new account with a small audience. One video that lands can reach thousands of Western students in London, Ontario without any existing audience.

The Western University student demographic skews younger — first and second year students who are the primary target are highly active on TikTok. The app suits the problem-solution format, the relatable scenario format, and the demo format CampusClip's content naturally fits.

**Key tactics:**
- First 3 seconds are everything — no slow builds
- Text overlays on every video (many watch without sound)
- Comment responses are content — answer comments in video replies when a comment lands well
- Use sound strategically — trending audio boosts discovery but only if it fits the video

### Platforms to Ignore (for now)

**LinkedIn:** Wrong audience for student life content. May be relevant if CampusClip wants to reach university administrators or investors — not relevant for student acquisition.

**Twitter/X:** Low student engagement in Canada for this demographic. Not worth the time investment.

**Facebook:** Western clubs have Facebook groups but student attention has moved. Monitor, don't invest.

## The Cold Start Solution — Full Strategy

The cold start problem in content is a chicken-and-egg loop: good social content requires users, but users require good social content. Here is the explicit strategy for breaking it.

**Step 1 — Launch with depth, not breadth.**
The account launches August 1 with a full week of pre-scheduled content already posted. First-time visitors see an active account with several posts, not an empty grid with one "we're coming soon" post.

**Step 2 — Make every August post shareable to a specific person.**
The most valuable share isn't "share to your story" — it's "send this to someone you know at Western." One post shared within a group chat of 20 Western students is worth more than 200 random impressions. Write captions that make the send natural: "send this to the person in your group chat who always asks when the assignment is due."

**Step 3 — Borrow existing audience through hashtags and location tags.**
Western students already use #WesternU and #UWO to find content about their campus. Every post tags into this existing discovery layer. The account doesn't need its own audience on day one — it borrows the existing audience looking for Western content.

**Step 4 — Comment-seed on Western accounts.**
Identify the 15 most active Western-adjacent accounts on Instagram (Western orientation, clubs, campus media, student associations). Become a consistent, genuine presence in their comments throughout August. This is not spam — it's showing up where the target audience already is.

**Step 5 — Use the app's own network as content.**
As soon as real students start using CampusClip, even in small numbers, that's content. "14 Western students joined CampusClip this week" is more compelling than any product claim. Actual usage numbers (even small) create social proof that the thing is real. Post them.

**Step 6 — Make early adopters feel like insiders, not beta testers.**
The language around early adoption should be about identity, not utility. "You were first at Western" is more powerful than "you got in early." Students who feel like they shaped how their campus uses something become advocates. Acknowledge them publicly. Reply to their comments. Make them visible.

## Voice and Tone Guardrails

These rules apply to every post, regardless of phase.

**Always:**
- Write like a second-year talking to a first-year
- Start with the feeling, not the feature
- Use short sentences
- Sound like one specific person wrote it, not a brand committee
- Use: scattered, all in one place, your classes, actually, just, finally, your grade, your campus

**Never:**
- Use: productivity, optimise, leverage, seamless, solution, platform, empower, revolutionise
- Speak from the brand's perspective ("we are excited to announce")
- Use corporate exclamation points ("We're LIVE!!!")
- Mention competitors by name
- Explain what the app does in every post — some posts can just be relatable

**The test:**
Read the caption out loud. If it sounds like something a specific 20-year-old at Western would say to a friend, it passes. If it sounds like a startup, rewrite it.
