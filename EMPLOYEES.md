# Marketing Skills — Employee Roster

Seven specialized employees, each owning a distinct slice of the marketing function. Every skill in this repository is assigned to exactly one employee.

---

## 1. SEO Specialist

**Owns**: Search visibility — technical, on-page, AI-era, and app store

| Skill | What they do |
|---|---|
| `seo-audit` | Diagnose technical and on-page SEO issues |
| `ai-seo` | Optimize content for AI search engines and LLM citations |
| `programmatic-seo` | Build keyword-targeted pages at scale |
| `schema-markup` | Implement structured data for rich results |
| `site-architecture` | Plan page hierarchy, URLs, and internal linking |
| `directory-submissions` | Submit to directories for backlinks and discovery |
| `aso-audit` | Optimize App Store and Google Play listings |

### Daily Responsibilities
- Monitor Google Search Console for crawl errors, coverage drops, and ranking shifts
- Check Core Web Vitals and page speed alerts
- Review AI search visibility (Perplexity, ChatGPT) for brand and target keyword mentions
- Respond to indexing issues flagged by automated audits
- Track new backlinks and flag suspicious or toxic links

### Weekly Responsibilities
- Run a full `seo-audit` pass on priority pages and log findings
- Update the `programmatic-seo` template pipeline with new keyword clusters
- Audit and add `schema-markup` to new or updated pages
- Submit new tools, blog posts, or product pages via `directory-submissions`
- Review `aso-audit` metrics (impressions, conversion rate, keyword rankings) for the app listing
- Sync with Content Strategist on upcoming publish dates to ensure pages are crawlable and internally linked
- Update `site-architecture` docs to reflect any new pages or navigation changes
- Check `ai-seo` citation presence and flag content gaps to the Content Strategist

## 2. Content Strategist & Writer

**Owns**: What to say, where to say it, and how to say it well

| Skill | What they do |
|---|---|
| `content-strategy` | Decide what content to create and build editorial calendars |
| `copywriting` | Write persuasive marketing copy for any page |
| `copy-editing` | Edit, polish, and refresh existing copy |
| `social-content` | Create and schedule social media content across platforms |
| `image` | Generate and optimize marketing visuals and graphics |
| `video` | Produce and generate video content using AI tools |

### Daily Responsibilities
- Write or edit at least one piece of content (blog post, landing page, or social post)
- Schedule and publish social content across active platforms using `social-content`
- Respond to content requests from other employees (e.g., ad copy drafts for Paid Media, page copy for CRO)
- Monitor content performance comments and engagement on published social posts
- Generate supporting visuals via `image` for any content going live that day

### Weekly Responsibilities
- Run a `content-strategy` session to plan the next two weeks of content and update the editorial calendar
- Complete a `copy-editing` sweep of the top five highest-traffic pages flagged by SEO Specialist
- Produce one long-form asset (blog post, guide, or case study) using `copywriting`
- Create one short-form video asset using `video` for distribution on social or email
- Sync with Product Marketer on messaging changes that need to cascade into web copy
- Deliver social content batch (5–7 posts) to the scheduling queue for the following week
- Review and refresh any copy flagged as outdated by the Analytics Analyst

## 3. Paid Media Manager

**Owns**: Paid acquisition — ads, creative, and launch moments

| Skill | What they do |
|---|---|
| `paid-ads` | Run Google, Meta, LinkedIn, and other ad campaigns |
| `ad-creative` | Generate and iterate ad headlines, copy, and variations |
| `launch-strategy` | Plan product launches and go-to-market releases |
| `marketing-ideas` | Brainstorm growth strategies and marketing tactics |
| `marketing-psychology` | Apply behavioral science and persuasion principles |

### Daily Responsibilities
- Check ad platform dashboards (Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn) for spend pacing, CPA, and ROAS
- Pause or adjust underperforming ad sets and reallocate budget to winning variants
- Flag creative fatigue when frequency rises and brief new variants using `ad-creative`
- Monitor comment sections on active social ads for sentiment and brand safety issues
- Log anomalies (CTR drops, CPC spikes) in the campaign health log

### Weekly Responsibilities
- Generate a new batch of ad creative variations using `ad-creative` for each active campaign
- Run a `paid-ads` optimization pass: review audience targeting, bids, negative keywords, and match types
- Apply `marketing-psychology` principles to review and sharpen one campaign's messaging angle
- Facilitate a `marketing-ideas` brainstorm session with the broader team to surface new channel or tactic experiments
- Produce a weekly paid media performance report (spend, leads, CPL, ROAS by channel)
- Coordinate with CRO Specialist on landing page performance tied to paid traffic
- Update `launch-strategy` docs for any upcoming product feature or campaign launch

## 4. CRO Specialist

**Owns**: Every conversion moment from first visit to upgrade

| Skill | What they do |
|---|---|
| `page-cro` | Optimize marketing pages for conversions |
| `signup-flow-cro` | Reduce friction in signup and registration flows |
| `onboarding-cro` | Improve post-signup activation and time-to-value |
| `form-cro` | Optimize lead capture, contact, and demo request forms |
| `popup-cro` | Create and optimize popups, modals, and overlays |
| `paywall-upgrade-cro` | Convert free users to paid with in-app upgrade screens |
| `ab-test-setup` | Design and run A/B tests and experimentation programs |

### Daily Responsibilities
- Check running A/B tests for statistical significance and flag winners or losers
- Monitor conversion funnels for drop-off anomalies (signup rate, activation rate, upgrade rate)
- Review heatmaps, session recordings, or form analytics for friction signals
- Respond to conversion-related questions from Paid Media (landing pages) and Demand Gen (email CTAs)
- Log new test hypotheses to the experiment backlog as insights emerge

### Weekly Responsibilities
- Launch at least one new A/B test using `ab-test-setup` from the prioritized experiment backlog
- Run a `page-cro` audit on the highest-traffic page that hasn't been reviewed in 30+ days
- Review the `signup-flow-cro` funnel step-by-step and identify the single highest-friction drop-off point
- Audit `onboarding-cro` metrics (activation rate, time to aha moment) and flag users getting stuck
- Optimize or rotate `popup-cro` and `form-cro` variants based on conversion data
- Review `paywall-upgrade-cro` screens for free-to-paid conversion rate and queue improvements
- Deliver a weekly CRO summary: active tests, concluded tests, CVR changes, and next priorities
- Sync with Analytics Analyst to validate tracking is firing correctly on all test variants

## 5. Demand Generation & Lifecycle Marketer

**Owns**: Lead generation, email, and community-driven growth

| Skill | What they do |
|---|---|
| `email-sequence` | Build drip campaigns and lifecycle email flows |
| `cold-email` | Write B2B cold outreach and follow-up sequences |
| `lead-magnets` | Create gated content for email capture |
| `free-tool-strategy` | Plan and build free tools for lead generation |
| `referral-program` | Design referral, affiliate, and ambassador programs |
| `community-marketing` | Build and leverage online communities for growth |

### Daily Responsibilities
- Monitor email deliverability metrics (open rate, click rate, bounce rate, spam complaints)
- Engage in community channels (Discord, Slack, Reddit, LinkedIn groups) — answer questions, surface user wins
- Review cold email reply rates and categorize responses (interested, not now, wrong fit)
- Check referral and affiliate dashboards for new signups, pending payouts, and fraud flags
- Respond to leads generated by free tools or lead magnets same day

### Weekly Responsibilities
- Audit one `email-sequence` flow end-to-end: open rates, click rates, drop-off points, and unsubscribes
- Write or refine one `cold-email` sequence for a new segment or persona
- Publish or promote one `lead-magnet` asset (new or refreshed) with a distribution push
- Review `free-tool-strategy` usage data: traffic, lead capture rate, and drop-off in the tool flow
- Run a `community-marketing` activity: host an AMA, post a discussion prompt, or spotlight a community member
- Review `referral-program` performance: referral rate, reward redemption, and top referrers
- Deliver a weekly pipeline report: new leads by source, MQL volume, email list growth, and community growth

## 6. Product Marketer

**Owns**: Positioning, pricing, competitive intelligence, and sales alignment

| Skill | What they do |
|---|---|
| `product-marketing-context` | Define and document product positioning and ICP |
| `pricing-strategy` | Set pricing tiers, packaging, and monetization model |
| `competitor-profiling` | Research and profile competitors from URLs |
| `competitor-alternatives` | Create comparison and alternative pages for SEO and sales |
| `sales-enablement` | Build pitch decks, one-pagers, and objection-handling docs |

### Daily Responsibilities
- Monitor competitor activity: new feature announcements, pricing changes, and positioning shifts
- Answer inbound questions from sales reps about objections, competitor comparisons, and deal-specific positioning
- Keep Slack/shared channel updated with any market intelligence gathered that day
- Review sales call recordings or summaries for recurring objections and messaging gaps

### Weekly Responsibilities
- Run a `competitor-profiling` update on the top three competitors — flag any new messaging or product changes
- Refresh or create one `competitor-alternatives` page based on SEO opportunity or sales request
- Update `product-marketing-context` doc with any positioning refinements from sales feedback or customer research
- Produce or update one `sales-enablement` asset (battle card, one-pager, objection handler, or demo script)
- Run a `pricing-strategy` review if any conversion, churn, or upgrade data suggests a packaging issue
- Sync with Content Strategist to ensure web copy reflects current positioning
- Sync with CRO Specialist to ensure pricing page and upgrade screens reflect current packaging
- Deliver a weekly competitive intelligence brief to the broader team

## 7. Analytics & Revenue Operations Analyst

**Owns**: Measurement, research, retention, and the marketing-to-revenue pipeline

| Skill | What they do |
|---|---|
| `analytics-tracking` | Set up and audit GA4, GTM, and conversion tracking |
| `customer-research` | Run interviews, surveys, and review mining |
| `revops` | Manage lead scoring, routing, and marketing-to-sales handoff |
| `churn-prevention` | Build cancellation flows, save offers, and retention systems |

### Daily Responsibilities
- Monitor the revenue and retention dashboard: MRR, churn rate, trial-to-paid, and failed payments
- Triage and route leads in the CRM — ensure MQLs are scored and handed off to sales within SLA
- Check `analytics-tracking` health: verify key events are firing and flag any broken tags or missing data
- Review cancellation survey responses and flag common churn reasons to the team
- Respond to ad-hoc data questions from other employees

### Weekly Responsibilities
- Run a `revops` audit: review lead scoring thresholds, routing rules, and pipeline stage definitions
- Audit `analytics-tracking` across the top 10 conversion events — fix any gaps with GTM
- Conduct or synthesize one `customer-research` activity: interview, survey send, or review mining session
- Review `churn-prevention` flows (cancellation page, save offers, dunning emails) for performance and update copy or triggers
- Deliver a weekly marketing performance report covering: traffic, leads, activations, conversions, churn, and MRR
- Share insights from customer research with Product Marketer and Content Strategist
- Sync with CRO Specialist to confirm test tracking is instrumented correctly before tests launch
